### General Assessment

**1. Working Environment**  
The working space at FCAJ is quite open and clean, making it easy to focus on work. Everyone on the team is friendly, doesn't create unnecessary pressure, and is always willing to answer questions, even the most basic ones. One suggestion would be to add a few small offline activities, such as weekend mini games, so interns have more chances to connect with one another, since most interaction currently happens through chat/online channels.

**2. Support from Mentors / Team Admin**  
The mentor is very patient, always taking the time to review work carefully and giving specific feedback to help improve gradually with each task. Rather than doing the work for the intern, the mentor tends to suggest a direction and lets the intern figure out the solution, which has helped build more independent thinking. On the admin side, support for matters like internship confirmation and work scheduling is quick and reliable, making it easier to stay focused on the actual work.

**3. Alignment Between the Work and Field of Study**  
Overall, the assigned work closely matches what was studied in school, while also introducing a few new areas involving technologies or tools that were previously only known in theory. This has been a good opportunity to put existing knowledge to the test and gain practical experience that isn't covered in depth at school.

**4. Learning Opportunities & Skill Development**  
Throughout the internship, it's been possible to build the habit of working with a plan, breaking down tasks, and tracking progress more clearly. Beyond technical skills, this experience also helped develop the ability to present ideas clearly when reporting to the mentor, as well as how to coordinate with other team members for smoother collaboration. The mentor's real-world sharing about the industry has also provided a clearer sense of direction going forward.

**5. Culture & Team Spirit**  
The work culture here is fairly open — people aren't afraid to share difficulties and are always willing to support one another when needed. Even as an intern, opinions are heard and there's no sense of being left out of work discussions. The general spirit is that everyone takes responsibility for their own part while still keeping things relaxed and not overly stressful.
